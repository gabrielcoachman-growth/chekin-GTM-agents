# Language Config — Greek (EL)

## Register
- **Formality:** Use `εσείς` (formal second person plural, equivalent to French `vous`) for B2B property manager audience — standard in Greek professional marketing
- Conjugate in second person plural formal: "αυτοματοποιείτε", "διαχειρίζεστε", "ξεκινήστε", "δοκιμάστε"
- Informal `εσύ` is acceptable in consumer-facing SaaS, but Chekin's B2B positioning in the Greek market warrants the formal register
- Never mix formal and informal on the same page

## Script and encoding
- Greek uses the Greek alphabet (Ελληνικό αλφάβητο) — verify that HTML encoding is `UTF-8` and that no Latin characters have been substituted for similar-looking Greek ones
- Common encoding errors to check: Greek text displaying as question marks or boxes, incorrect polytonic marks
- Monotonic Greek is the modern standard — do not use polytonic (ancient) accents in modern web copy

## Chekin voice — Greek adaptation
- **Problem-first framing:** "Ο χειροκίνητος check-in σας κοστίζει χρόνο" before "Με το Chekin, οι επισκέπτες σας κάνουν check-in μόνοι τους"
- **Outcome-led language:** "Οι επισκέπτες σας κάνουν check-in αυτόνομα" — the Greek equivalent of "funcione solo"
- **Brand name:** In Greek, foreign brand names take a Greek article based on implied gender. "Chekin" is typically treated as neuter: "το Chekin". In headings, omit the article for brand-style consistency. In body copy, "το Chekin" or "η πλατφόρμα Chekin" are both acceptable
- **Tone:** Greek B2B copy is warmer and more relational than Northern European equivalents but still professional. Avoid overly cold or mechanical phrasing

## Greek regulatory context
- **Μητρώο Ακινήτων Βραχυχρόνιας Μίσθωσης (ΜΑΒΜ)** — Greek short-term rental registry. Use exact term or the abbreviation ΜΑΒΜ when referencing compliance
- **ΑΑΔΕ** (Ανεξάρτητη Αρχή Δημοσίων Εσόδων) — Greek tax authority. Use when referencing tax compliance
- **ΑΜΑ** (Αριθμός Μητρώου Ακινήτου) — property registry number required for all STR listings in Greece
- If the page references Greek compliance, use these official terms — do not translate or generalize them

## Common translation pitfalls to fix
- **"Καλώς ήρθατε στο Chekin"** as headline → rewrite to outcome-led
- **"Η πλατφόρμα μας προσφέρει..."** → "Με το Chekin, οι επισκέπτες σας..."
- **"Κάντε κλικ εδώ"** → "Ξεκινήστε δωρεάν", "Δοκιμάστε τώρα", "Δείτε πώς λειτουργεί"
- **"Παρακαλώ συμπληρώστε τη φόρμα"** → "Συμπληρώστε τη φόρμα"
- **"Λύση"** as standalone noun → replace with specific outcome
- **Monotonic accent errors:** Every Greek word of more than one syllable must have exactly one accent mark (τόνος). Missing accents are immediately noticeable to Greek readers. Check that the translation has not stripped accents.
- **Incorrect article-noun agreement:** Greek nouns have three genders (masculine, feminine, neuter) and four cases. Articles must agree. Machine translations frequently produce gender or case mismatches.
- **Latinized transliterations:** Some machine translations output Greek words in Latin script (Greeklish) — never acceptable in published web copy. Flag and rewrite.

## SEO — Greek market patterns
- Greek STR market is heavily tourism-driven — islands, Athens, Thessaloniki
- Primary search intent: "online check-in κατάλυμα", "βραχυχρόνια μίσθωση διαχείριση", "αυτόματο check-in"
- Greek users search in Greek — meta content must be fully in Greek (except established English terms like "check-in")
- ΑΜΑ / ΜΑΒΜ compliance has active search volume among Greek property owners and managers
- Greece is a seasonal market — if pages reference seasonal management, the framing should reflect this
- Meta description CTAs: "Δοκιμάστε δωρεάν", "Μάθετε περισσότερα", "Ξεκινήστε τώρα"

## Quality check — native speaker test
Read the H1, subheadline, and first paragraph. Ask: would a Greek property manager in Athens or Heraklion naturally write this? Key tells: missing accent marks, incorrect gender/case agreement, Latinized text mixed into Greek, and sentences that follow English structure rather than natural Greek word order.
