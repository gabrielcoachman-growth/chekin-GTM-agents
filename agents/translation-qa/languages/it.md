# Language Config — Italian (IT)

## Register
- **Formality:** Use `tu` (informal) for property manager and host audiences — standard in Italian SaaS and hospitality marketing
- Conjugate in second person singular: "automatizza", "gestisci", "inizia", "prova"
- Exception: if the page targets enterprise hotel chains or large hotel groups, switch to `Lei` (formal) — indicated in filename or folder (`/hotel-enterprise/`)
- Do NOT use `voi` as a formal second-person alternative — archaic in modern Italian marketing
- Never mix registers on the same page

## Chekin voice — Italian adaptation
- **Problem-first framing:** "Il check-in manuale ti fa perdere tempo" before "Con Chekin, i tuoi ospiti fanno il check-in da soli"
- **Outcome-led language:** "I tuoi ospiti si registrano autonomamente" — the Italian equivalent of "funcione solo"
- **Brand name:** No article before Chekin in headings. In body copy, "Chekin" without article is preferred in marketing contexts
- **Tone:** Italian B2B copy is warmer and more relational than German or French. It can be slightly more expressive but should not be overly effusive. Avoid superlatives like "straordinario", "rivoluzionario" — they ring hollow
- **Brevity:** Italian sentences tend to be long and subordinated. Cut subordinate clauses in headings and CTAs

## Common translation pitfalls to fix
- **"Benvenuto su Chekin"** as headline → rewrite to outcome-led
- **"La nostra piattaforma offre..."** → "Con Chekin, i tuoi ospiti..."
- **"Clicca qui"** → "Inizia gratis", "Prova ora", "Scopri come funziona"
- **"Si prega di compilare il modulo"** → "Compila il modulo" (direct, informal)
- **"Soluzione"** as standalone noun → replace with specific outcome
- **Passive overuse:** "viene offerto", "viene gestito" → rewrite to active
- **Gerund overuse:** "Offrendo una soluzione..." → "Chekin automatizza..." (direct subject)
- **False friends to catch:**
  - "attuale" = current (not actual) — "actual" in English = "effettivo" or "vero" in Italian
  - "eventuale" = possible/potential, not eventual
  - "assumere" = to hire, not to assume
  - "sensibile" = sensitive or significant (not sensible)
  - "confrontare" = to compare, not to confront
- **"Funzionalità"** as a heading → too generic. Replace with specific outcome
- **Double negatives in Italian** are grammatically correct ("non ho niente") but avoid them in marketing copy — rewrite positively

## Italian regulatory context (for compliance pages)
- **Alloggiati Web** — mandatory police registration system for Italian accommodations. Use exact name.
- **CIR** (Codice Identificativo di Riferimento) — Italian short-term rental registration code. Use exact acronym.
- **Tassa di soggiorno** — tourist tax, managed at municipal level. Correct term.
- If these are referenced, do not translate or generalize them — use the official names

## SEO — Italian market patterns
- Primary search intent: "check-in online affitti brevi", "automatizzare check-in", "gestione affitti brevi"
- "Affitti brevi" is the dominant Italian industry term — ensure it appears in meta description for relevant pages
- "Struttura ricettiva" has high search volume for B&B and hotel-adjacent searches
- Italian users search in Italian — do not leave English keyword phrases in meta tags
- Local compliance terms (CIR, Alloggiati Web, tassa di soggiorno) have search volume and should appear in relevant pages' meta descriptions
- Meta description CTAs: "Prova gratis", "Scopri di più", "Inizia ora"

## Quality check — native speaker test
Read the H1, subheadline, and first paragraph. Ask: would a property manager in Milan or Rome naturally write this? Key tells: incorrect gender agreement, overuse of passive voice, false friends, and sentences that are clearly structured on English syntax.
